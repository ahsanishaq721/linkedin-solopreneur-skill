# Justin Welsh -- Complete Strategy Frameworks

## Framework 1: Origin Mindset -- Burnout = Loss of Control
Burnout is not about working too hard. It happens when you lose control.
Every business decision must protect autonomy. When an offer threatens control -- redesign it, regardless of revenue potential.

## Framework 2: Niche of One
"The niche is me. When the niche is me, I don't compete with anyone else."
Your Niche of One = Your expertise x Your delivery method x Your business model x Your unique POV.
Generic content is a commodity. Your specific workflows, failures, wins, and opinions are not.
Formula: [Skill/Tool] x [Industry/Audience] x [Your unique angle]

## Framework 3: Lifestyle First, Business Second
Revenue is a vanity metric. Profit margin is the real score.
- A $1M business at 90% margin beats a $5M business at 20% -- every time
- Know your "enough" number
- Design every offer so you can disappear for a week without anyone depending on you

## Framework 4: The Bleeding Neck Problem
Every post, offer, and DM must be anchored to a pain so urgent the prospect needs a solution NOW.
Ask before every piece of content:
- What is the specific pain?
- How urgent is it?
- Why is NOW the moment to act?
Classic example: "I'm spending 20 hours/week on tasks a workflow could handle in 20 minutes."

## Framework 5: Service Before Product
Sequence: 1:1 Service (5-10 clients) -> find patterns -> build framework -> productize -> launch with survey data.
- Never launch a course before solving the problem manually for 20 people
- Pre-launch method: Carrd landing page -> A/B survey questions -> build exactly what audience wants

## Framework 6: Moneyball Consistency
"Get on base every day."
- One post per day
- One platform mastered first
- Small consistent actions compound into authority
- Do not chase viral home runs

## Framework 7: The Creator Funnel
DISCOVERY -> TRUST -> DEEPEN -> MONETIZE
(LinkedIn) -> (Newsletter) -> (Community) -> (Offers)
- Social media = discovery only
- Newsletter = expertise and authority hub
- Community = required only for offers $1,000+

## Framework 8: Profile Architecture
Profile answers ONE question: "Why should I follow this person?"
- Headline: [Who you help] + [How] + [Result/Proof]. No job title. ~120 chars.
- About: Pain -> Agitate -> Solution -> Social proof -> CTA to newsletter
- Featured: Max 2 items. Item 1 (Free): newsletter. Item 2 (Paid): audit/product
- Banner: What you do (5 words) + Where to go + Social proof

## Framework 9: Post Anatomy
Write MEAT first. Hook second. Always.
TRAILER (<=210 chars): Line 1 scroll-stopper -> Line 2 validator -> Line 3 hook
MEAT: actual teaching/story/teardown
CTC: Recap + question OR CTA: direct action
VISUAL: Carousel > Infographic > 4x5 graphic

## Framework 10: PAIPS Formula
P -- PAIN: "Most agency owners spend 20 hrs/week on manual tasks..."
A -- AGITATE: "That's 20 hours NOT spent on growth, strategy, or sleep."
I -- INTRIGUE: "What if your entire ops stack ran itself?"
P -- POSITIVE FUTURE: "Imagine reviewing 3 dashboards instead of doing 50 tasks."
S -- SOLUTION: "Here's the system I built that does exactly that."

## Framework 11: Content Matrix
Cross Themes x Styles every Saturday. Write 10 headlines. Pick best 5.
Themes: your core tool builds, AI agents, agency ops, client acquisition, lifestyle business, failures/lessons, tool comparisons
Styles: Teaching, Observation, Prediction, Comparison, Teardown, Listicle, Contrarian

## Framework 12: Play the Hits
- Track every post with Shield App
- When one performs: create 5 variations, stagger one per month
- New people arrive daily. Old followers forget. Repeat your best work.

## Framework 13: Newsletter Hub-and-Spoke
One newsletter = 10-20 content pieces
Friday -> Pre-CTA | Saturday -> Send | Sunday -> Post-CTA
Monday -> Twitter thread | Tuesday -> LinkedIn carousel
Weeks 1-6 -> 6-8 short posts plugging newsletter

## Framework 14: Newsletter Writing Formula (75 mins)
1. Pull topic from Idea Bank
2. Research 2-3 supporting pieces
3. Template: Big problem -> why common solution fails -> your solution -> action step
4. Write HEADERS first
5. Fill body
6. Insert visuals
7. Write title LAST
8. Edit to Grade 5 level (Hemingway App)

## Framework 15: The 7:50 AM Strategy
1. Identify 10 Giants in adjacent niches
2. Turn on post notifications
3. Comment within first 15 minutes
Rules: No "Great post!" -- Spiky, specific, contrarian. 2-4 sentences.

## Framework 16: De-Platforming
LinkedIn is rented land. Email list is owned land.
- Every 3rd post has newsletter CTA
- Featured section = always lead capture
- Make sign-up feel like receiving something

## Framework 17: Productization Ladder + FITI
Level 1: $100-500 audits -> testimonials
Level 2: $500-2,500 productized service
Level 3: $3,000+ retainer
FITI: Feedback -> Iteration -> Testimonials -> Increase prices
Trust Tripwire: First product $50-$150. Deliver 100x value.
PPP Pricing: 80% discount for Pakistan, India, Nigeria, Mexico etc.

## Framework 18: ESAD Framework
E -- ELIMINATE: Not in top 20% goals? Cut it.
S -- SIMPLIFY: Can this be done in fewer steps?
A -- AUTOMATE: Can your automation stack handle this?
D -- DELEGATE: Can a VA handle what's left?

## Framework 21: Strategic Networking
Connection Request (300 chars): Specific observation + soft opener. Zero pitch.
First DM: Value first. No ask. Open question.
Follow-up DM: New unrelated insight. Zero pressure.
Launch Activation: One ask per year.
