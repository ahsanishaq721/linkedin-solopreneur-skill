# Justin Welsh -- Complete Strategy Frameworks (Generic Edition)

All 21 frameworks apply to any niche, profession, or industry.
Load this file when the user asks about a specific framework or wants a strategy deep-dive.

---

## Framework 1: Origin Mindset -- Burnout = Loss of Control
Burnout is not about working too hard. It happens when you lose control.
Every business decision must protect autonomy. When an offer threatens control -- redesign it, regardless of revenue potential.
Key question to ask: "Does this opportunity give me more control or less?"

## Framework 2: Niche of One
"The niche is me. When the niche is me, I don't compete with anyone else."
Formula: [Your core expertise] x [Your delivery method] x [Your business model] x [Your unique POV]
Generic content is a commodity. Your specific experiences, frameworks, failures, wins, and opinions are not.
Examples:
- Executive coach x async video feedback x subscription x contrarian leadership takes
- Copywriter x landing pages only x productized service x direct response purist
- Financial advisor x young professionals x flat-fee model x anti-Wall-Street POV

## Framework 3: Lifestyle First, Business Second
Revenue is a vanity metric. Profit margin is the real score.
- A $1M business at 90% margin beats a $5M business at 20% -- every time
- Know your "enough" number (monthly income that lets you live how you want)
- Design every offer so you can disappear for a week without anyone depending on you
Test: Can your business run without you for 7 days? If not, redesign it.

## Framework 4: The Bleeding Neck Problem
Every post, offer, and DM must be anchored to a pain so urgent the prospect needs a solution NOW.
Ask before every piece of content:
- What is the most specific pain my audience feels daily?
- How urgent is it? (bleeding neck = cannot wait)
- Why is NOW the moment they need to act?
Rule: Always diagnose before prescribing. Never lead with your solution. Lead with their pain.

## Framework 5: Service Before Product
Sequence: 1:1 Service (5-10 clients) -> find patterns -> build framework -> productize -> launch with survey data
- Never launch a digital product before solving the problem manually for at least 20 people
- Pre-launch method: simple landing page -> survey questions -> analyze answers -> build exactly what audience wants
- Real data from real clients beats assumptions every time

## Framework 6: Moneyball Consistency
"Get on base every day."
- One post per day on LinkedIn
- One platform mastered before expanding to others
- Small consistent actions compound into authority over 12-24 months
- Do not chase viral home runs -- singles win the game
Success metric: Did I show up today? Not: Did I go viral?

## Framework 7: The Creator Funnel
DISCOVERY -> TRUST -> DEEPEN -> MONETIZE
(LinkedIn) -> (Newsletter) -> (Community) -> (Offers)
- LinkedIn = discovery only. Do not try to sell here directly.
- Newsletter = your expertise hub. This is where authority is built.
- Community = only needed for offers at $1,000 or higher
- Keep it simple: no email sequences, no complex funnels, no complexity

## Framework 8: Profile Architecture (Sales Page, Not Resume)
Your LinkedIn profile answers ONE question: "Why should I follow this person?"
- Headline: [Who you help] + [How] + [Result or Proof]. No job title. ~120 characters.
- About: Pain -> Agitate -> Solution -> Social proof -> CTA to newsletter. Keywords from your niche throughout.
- Featured: Max 2 items. Item 1 (Free): newsletter or lead magnet. Item 2 (Paid): your main offer. No descriptions on either -- reduce friction.
- Banner: What you do in 5 words + Where to go + One line of social proof. Bold and simple.

## Framework 9: Post Anatomy
Write MEAT first. Hook second. Always. Never the other way around.
Structure:
TRAILER (<=210 chars above "...more"): scroll-stopper -> validator -> hook
MEAT: actual teaching / story / teardown
CTC: Recap + question (drives comments)
OR
CTA: Direct action prompt (drives newsletter signups or link clicks)
VISUAL: Carousel PDF > Infographic > 4x5 graphic
Rule: Visuals increase dwell time -- the #1 LinkedIn algorithm signal.

## Framework 10: PAIPS Formula
Use for story posts, About sections, newsletter hooks, and outreach DMs.
P -- PAIN:            State the specific daily pain your audience lives with
A -- AGITATE:         Show the full cost of NOT solving it (lost time, money, stress, opportunity)
I -- INTRIGUE:        Hint at a better alternative without revealing it fully
P -- POSITIVE FUTURE: Paint a vivid picture of life once the problem is solved
S -- SOLUTION:        Introduce your method, framework, offer, or approach
Rule: Make each step specific to the user's niche and audience. Generic PAIPS is useless.

## Framework 11: Content Matrix
Cross Themes x Styles every week. Write 10 headlines. Pick the best 5.

Universal Themes (adapt to any niche):
- Your core expertise process (how you do what you do)
- Client transformations and real results
- The most common mistake your audience makes
- Industry trends and where things are heading
- Your personal story -- failures, pivots, lessons
- Tool, method, or approach comparisons
- Contrarian takes on conventional wisdom in your field

Styles:
- Teaching: step-by-step process breakdown
- Observation: what most people get wrong
- Prediction: where your industry is heading
- Comparison: two approaches side by side
- Teardown: real example analyzed in detail
- Listicle: curated list of X things
- Contrarian: unpopular but defensible opinion

## Framework 12: Play the Hits
- Track every post with Shield App (LinkedIn analytics)
- When a post performs well: create 5 variations, stagger them one per month
- Build a library of 90 "hits" so 25% of a year's content is repurposed proven work
- New people find your profile every day. Old followers forget. Repeating top posts is smart, not lazy.

## Framework 13: Newsletter Hub-and-Spoke
One newsletter issue generates 10-20 separate content pieces.
Friday -> Pre-CTA post (tease the topic)
Saturday -> Send the newsletter
Sunday -> Post-CTA post (tell people to subscribe if they missed it)
Monday -> Twitter/X thread from newsletter
Tuesday -> LinkedIn carousel from thread screenshots
Weeks 1-6 -> 6-8 short-form LinkedIn posts, each plugging the newsletter
Result: One issue of writing = 2-3 weeks of content across platforms.

## Framework 14: Newsletter Writing Formula (75 mins total)
Step 1: Pull topic from Idea Bank (Notion database, add 2-3 ideas daily)
Step 2: Research -- find 2-3 supporting pieces (quotes, data, case studies)
Step 3: Structure: Big problem -> why common solution fails -> your solution (4-5 tips) -> action step
Step 4: Write HEADERS first -- creates a mini-outline that prevents rambling
Step 5: Fill in body -- intent line, insight, action step per section
Step 6: Add visuals and research citations
Step 7: Write the title/subject line LAST -- test with a subject line tester tool
Step 8: Edit to Grade 5 reading level using Hemingway App
Monetization paths: Sponsorships ($1,500+/issue at scale), product soft mentions, paid subscription tier ($9/mo), affiliate links.

## Framework 15: The 7:50 AM Strategy (Strategic Commenting)
Step 1: Identify 10 Giants -- large accounts in adjacent niches (not your direct competitors)
Step 2: Turn on LinkedIn post notifications for all 10
Step 3: Comment within the first 15 minutes of every post they publish
Comment rules:
- Never say "Great post!" or "I agree!" -- these are invisible
- Write 2-4 sentences with a spiky, specific, contrarian, or additive point of view
- Optionally end with a question that invites dialogue
- Zero self-promotion in the comment itself
Result: A top comment on a post with 100K+ views = thousands of free profile visits.

## Framework 16: De-Platforming (Own Your Audience)
LinkedIn is rented land. Your email list is owned land.
Rules:
- Every 3rd post should include a newsletter CTA
- Featured section = always a lead capture, never just a portfolio link
- Make signing up feel like receiving something valuable ("Get my free [X] when you subscribe")
- Goal: move every LinkedIn follower to your email list permanently

## Framework 17: Productization Ladder + FITI
Level 1: Low-cost service ($100-500) -> get clients, get testimonials, find patterns
Level 2: Mid-cost productized service ($500-2,500) -> niche down, reduce scope, increase consistency
Level 3: High-cost retainer ($3,000+) -> micro-niche, premium experience, few clients
FITI cycle: Feedback -> Iteration -> Testimonials -> Increase prices. Repeat.
Trust Tripwire: First digital product priced at $50-$150. Deliver so much value they feel guilty paying that little. This builds a buyer base guaranteed to purchase the next offer.
PPP Pricing: Offer 80% discounts for buyers in lower-income countries. This is smart marketing -- 10,000 more customers generating referrals and word of mouth.

## Framework 18: ESAD Framework (Time Management)
Value your time at $1,000/hour. Run every task through:
E -- ELIMINATE: Is this in my top 20% highest-value activities? If not -- cut it entirely.
S -- SIMPLIFY:  Can this be done in fewer steps without losing quality?
A -- AUTOMATE:  Can any tool, software, or AI handle this?
D -- DELEGATE:  Can a virtual assistant handle what remains? (~20 hrs/week of delegation is ideal)
Target: Spend your own time only on things that require your unique expertise.

## Framework 21: Strategic Networking
Connection Request (300 chars max):
  Specific observation about their work + a soft, curiosity-based opener. Zero pitch.
First DM:
  Lead with genuine value -- an insight, resource, or observation relevant to them. No ask. End with one open question.
Follow-up DM:
  A completely new, unrelated insight or resource. Still no ask. Zero pressure.
Launch Activation:
  One direct ask per year. Spend all year building trust and goodwill. Make one well-timed, well-prepared offer. Let word-of-mouth carry it.
