# Writing Templates, Formulas & Examples

All templates are niche-agnostic. Replace [brackets] with your specific context.
Load this file when executing /post, /newsletter, /profile, or /outreach commands.

---

## POST TEMPLATES

### Template 1: Teaching Post
TRAILER (<=210 chars):
[Counterintuitive statement about your topic -- 1 line]
[Who this affects and why it matters -- 1 line]
[What you are about to reveal -- 1 line]

MEAT:
Here's exactly how [process or method] works:

1. [Step with a specific, concrete detail]
2. [Step with a specific, concrete detail]
3. [Step with a specific, concrete detail]
4. [Step with a specific, concrete detail]

Result: [Specific outcome with a number or timeframe]

CTC: What's your current approach to [topic]?

---

### Template 2: Story Post (PAIPS)
TRAILER (<=210 chars):
[The final outcome -- start at the end, not the beginning]
[The stakes -- what was at risk]
[The hook -- why you're sharing this]

MEAT (follow PAIPS structure):
Pain: [The specific problem you or your client faced]
Agitate: [The full cost of that problem -- time, money, stress, missed opportunity]
Intrigue: [Hint at what changed without revealing it yet]
Positive Future: [What life looked like once the problem was solved]
Solution: [The specific thing that made the difference]

CTC: Have you ever faced [the same or similar situation]?

---

### Template 3: Contrarian Post
TRAILER (<=210 chars):
[Challenge a widely believed piece of advice in your field]
[Why believing this is actually costing people]
[The alternative you are about to argue for]

MEAT:
Everyone in [your field] tells you to [common conventional advice].

Here's why that's wrong:

[Reason 1 -- specific, not vague]
[Reason 2 -- specific, not vague]
[Reason 3 -- specific, not vague]

The truth: [Your contrarian position stated clearly and confidently]

CTC: Do you agree -- or am I missing something?

---

### Template 4: Listicle Post
TRAILER (<=210 chars):
[Number] things [specific audience] wish they knew about [specific topic]:
(Most people only know the first [lower number])

MEAT:
[Item 1 -- 1-2 lines, specific and useful]
[Item 2 -- 1-2 lines, specific and useful]
[Item 3 -- 1-2 lines, specific and useful]
[Item 4 -- 1-2 lines, specific and useful]
[Item 5 -- 1-2 lines, specific and useful]

Aim for 5-7 items. Each must stand alone as a useful insight.

CTC: Which of these surprised you most?

---

### Template 5: Teardown Post
TRAILER (<=210 chars):
I [analyzed / audited / rebuilt / tested] [specific thing].
Here's exactly what I found:
[The most surprising or counterintuitive result]

MEAT:
Setup: [What you started with and why]
What I did: [The specific action you took]
What happened: [The real result -- include numbers where possible]
What I learned: [The insight that others can apply]

CTC: Have you tried [the approach]? What did you find?

## OUTREACH TEMPLATES

### Connection Request (300 characters max)
Saw your [post / talk / article] on [specific topic] -- [one specific observation about what they said or did].
Working in a similar space around [your niche]. Would love to connect.

---

### First DM (Value-First, No Ask)
Hey [Name],

[One specific observation about their work, content, or company -- not generic praise.]

Thought of you when I came across [specific insight, resource, or tool] -- [one sentence on why it's relevant to them specifically].

[One open question that invites a reply -- not a sales question.]

---

### Follow-Up DM (New Insight, Zero Pressure)
Hey [Name], circling back --

[A completely new, unrelated insight or resource that is genuinely useful to them. 1-2 sentences.]

[Optional: one soft open question. No pitch. No ask.]

---

## NEWSLETTER TEMPLATE

SUBJECT LINE: [Write last. Test with a subject line tester. Aim for curiosity or specificity.]

OPENING (3-5 lines):
[Pattern interrupt -- an unexpected statement or counterintuitive fact]
[Why this topic matters right now]
[What the reader will get from reading this issue]

---

SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM
[State the specific, urgent problem your audience faces. Be concrete. No vague generalities.]

---

SECTION 2: WHY THE COMMON SOLUTION FAILS
[Explain why the advice they usually hear doesn't actually solve it. This builds trust.]

---

SECTION 3: YOUR SOLUTION
[Your framework, method, or insight broken into 4-5 clear, actionable tips.]

Tip 1: [Headline] -- [2-3 lines of explanation]
Tip 2: [Headline] -- [2-3 lines of explanation]
Tip 3: [Headline] -- [2-3 lines of explanation]
Tip 4: [Headline] -- [2-3 lines of explanation]

---

SECTION 4: ACTION STEP
[One thing the reader can do THIS WEEK to apply what they just learned. Specific and small.]

---

CLOSING:
[1-line recap of the main insight]
[Soft mention of your product, service, or offer -- not a hard sell]
[Sign-off]

## PROFILE TEMPLATES

### Headline Formula
I help [specific audience] [achieve specific, measurable result] [using your method, framework, or approach]

Examples (generic -- adapt to any niche):
- I help first-time managers lead teams without burning out -- in 90 days
- I help e-commerce founders write copy that converts without hiring an agency
- I help solo consultants land $5K+ clients using one simple LinkedIn system

---

### About Section Structure
[PAIN -- 2 lines: The specific problem your audience lives with daily]

[AGITATE -- 1-2 lines: The cost of that problem staying unsolved]

[SOLUTION -- 2-3 lines: How you solve it, what makes your approach different]

[SOCIAL PROOF -- 1-2 lines: Specific result, number of clients, years of experience, or a short testimonial]

[CTA -- 1 line: Subscribe to [Your Newsletter Name]: [link]]

## VISUAL GUIDE

Post type               | Best visual format
------------------------|-----------------------------------
Teaching / Steps        | Carousel PDF (swipe-through slides)
Comparison              | Side-by-side infographic
Stats or data           | Single bold stat on a 4x5 graphic
Story                   | No visual needed (text performs)
Teardown                | Screenshot + annotation overlay
List                    | Numbered graphic or carousel
