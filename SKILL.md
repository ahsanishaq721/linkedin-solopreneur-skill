## CORE FRAMEWORKS

### 1. ORIGIN MINDSET - Burnout = Loss of Control
Burnout is not about working too hard. It happens when you lose control. Every business decision must protect autonomy. When an offer threatens control -- redesign it, regardless of revenue potential.

### 2. NICHE OF ONE
"The niche is me. When the niche is me, I don't compete with anyone else."
Niche of One formula: [Your core expertise] x [Your delivery method] x [Your business model] x [Your unique POV].
Generic content is a commodity. Specific experiences, failures, wins, and opinions are not. Apply this to the user's niche specifically.

### 3. LIFESTYLE FIRST, BUSINESS SECOND
Revenue is a vanity metric. Profit margin is the real score. A $1M business at 90% margin beats a $5M business at 20% -- every time. Know your "enough" number. Design every offer so you can disappear for a week without anyone depending on you.

### 4. THE BLEEDING NECK PROBLEM
Every post, offer, and DM must be anchored to a pain so urgent the prospect needs a solution NOW. Before writing anything, identify the user's audience's most urgent pain. Frame every output around that pain. Always diagnose before prescribing.

### 5. SERVICE BEFORE PRODUCT
Sequence: 1:1 Service (5-10 clients) -> find patterns -> build framework -> productize -> launch with survey data. Never launch a course before solving the problem manually for 20 people. Pre-launch: Carrd landing page -> survey -> build exactly what the audience wants.

### 6. MONEYBALL CONSISTENCY
"Get on base every day." One post per day. One platform mastered first. Small consistent actions compound into authority. Do not chase viral home runs.

### 7. THE CREATOR FUNNEL
DISCOVERY -> TRUST -> DEEPEN -> MONETIZE
(LinkedIn)   (Newsletter)  (Community)  (Offers)

Social media = discovery only. Newsletter = expertise and authority. Community = required only for offers $1,000+. No email sequences. No complexity.

### 8. PROFILE ARCHITECTURE (Sales Page, Not Resume)
Profile answers ONE question: "Why should I follow this person?"

- **Headline:** [Who you help] + [How] + [Result/Proof]. No job title. ~120 chars.
- **About:** Pain -> Agitate -> Solution -> Social proof -> CTA to newsletter.
- **Featured:** Max 2 items. Item 1 (Free): newsletter signup. Item 2 (Paid): audit/product/service.
- **Banner:** What you do (5 words) + Where to go + Social proof. Bold, simple.

### 9. POST ANATOMY
Write MEAT first. Hook second. Always.

TRAILER (<=210 chars above "...more"):
  Line 1: Scroll-stopper
  Line 2: Validator
  Line 3: Hook (earns the "...more" click)

MEAT: The actual teaching / story / teardown

CTC: Recap + question ("Do you agree?") -- drives comments
OR
CTA: Direct action ("Link in comments") -- drives signups

VISUAL: Carousel (PDF) > Infographic > 4x5 graphic
Visuals increase dwell time = #1 LinkedIn algorithm signal

### 10. PAIPS FORMULA
Use for story posts, About sections, newsletter hooks, and DMs.

P -- PAIN:            State the specific pain your audience feels daily
A -- AGITATE:         Show the cost of NOT solving it (time, money, stress)
I -- INTRIGUE:        Hint at a better way without revealing it yet
P -- POSITIVE FUTURE: Paint the outcome if the problem were solved
S -- SOLUTION:        Introduce your method, framework, or service

Adapt all five steps to the user's niche and audience.

### 11. THE CONTENT MATRIX
Cross Themes x Styles every week. Write 10 headlines. Pick best 5.

**User's Themes** (derived from their niche -- generate these based on their context):
- Core expertise topics (what they know best)
- Client results and transformations
- Common mistakes their audience makes
- Industry trends and predictions
- Behind the scenes of their process
- Failures and lessons learned
- Tool or method comparisons

**Styles:**
- Teaching -- step-by-step process breakdown
- Observation -- what most people get wrong
- Prediction -- where things are heading
- Comparison -- two approaches side by side
- Teardown -- real example analyzed
- Listicle -- curated list of X things
- Contrarian -- unpopular but defensible opinion

### 12. PLAY THE HITS
Track every post with Shield App. When one performs: create 5 variations, stagger one per month. Build 90 "hits" so 25% of a year's content is repurposed. New people arrive daily. Old followers forget. Repeat your best work.

### 13. NEWSLETTER HUB-AND-SPOKE
One newsletter issue = 10-20 content pieces:
Step 1: Write newsletter (75 mins)
Step 2: Friday -> Pre-CTA (tease the topic)
Step 3: Saturday -> Send newsletter
Step 4: Sunday -> Post-CTA (tell people to sign up if they missed it)
Step 5: Monday -> Twitter/X thread from newsletter
Step 6: Tuesday -> Thread screenshots -> LinkedIn carousel
Step 7: Weeks 1-6 -> 6-8 short-form posts from the same issue
Step 8: Each post plugs the newsletter -> drives new subscribers

### 14. NEWSLETTER WRITING FORMULA (75 mins)
Step 1: Pull topic from Idea Bank
Step 2: Research -- 2-3 supporting pieces (quotes, data, examples)
Step 3: Template: Big problem -> why common solution fails -> your solution (4-5 tips) -> action step
Step 4: Write HEADERS first (mini-outline, prevents tangents)
Step 5: Fill body -- intent line -> insights -> action step per section
Step 6: Insert visuals and research
Step 7: Write title LAST -- test with email subject line tester
Step 8: Edit to Grade 5 reading level using Hemingway App

Newsletter monetization: Sponsorships ($1,500/issue at scale), product funnel (soft mention at end), $9/mo subscription tier, affiliate resource page.

### 15. THE 7:50 AM STRATEGY (Strategic Commenting)
Identify 10 Giants (large accounts in adjacent niches). Turn on post notifications. Comment within the first 15 minutes of their post going live.

Rules: No "Great post!" No "I agree!" Instead: spiky, specific, contrarian point of view that adds to or respectfully challenges the post. 2-4 sentences. A top comment on a 100K-view post = thousands of free profile visits.

### 16. DE-PLATFORMING
LinkedIn is rented land. Email list is owned land. Move followers off LinkedIn permanently. Every 3rd post has a newsletter CTA. Featured section = always a lead capture. Make sign-up feel like receiving something of value.

### 17. PRODUCTIZATION LADDER + FITI
Level 1: Low-cost service ($100-500) -> learn + get testimonials
Level 2: Mid-cost productized service ($500-2,500) -> niche down, focus
Level 3: High-cost retainer ($3,000+) -> micro-niche, premium delivery

FITI: Feedback -> Iteration -> Testimonials -> Increase (prices).

Trust Tripwire: First digital product at $50-$150. Deliver 100x perceived value. Build a customer base guaranteed to buy the next offer.
PPP Pricing: 80% discount for buyers in lower-income countries (Pakistan, India, Nigeria, Mexico, etc.) -- not charity, it's 10,000 more customers generating referrals.

### 18. ESAD FRAMEWORK
Value your time at $1,000/hour. Run every task through:

S -- SIMPLIFY:  Can this be done in fewer steps?
A -- AUTOMATE:  Can a tool or software handle this?
D -- DELEGATE:  Can a VA handle what's left? (~20 hrs/week)

## COMMAND PROTOCOLS

### /post [topic]
Check user context is set. Then:
1. Identify the bleeding neck angle for their specific audience
2. Write MEAT first (the actual teaching, story, or teardown)
3. Write TRAILER (<=210 chars): scroll-stopper -> validator -> hook
4. Write CTC (drives comments) OR CTA (drives newsletter signups) based on goal
5. Apply PAIPS structure if story-style
6. Suggest visual format
7. Load references/templates.md for exact template structure

Output format:
```
--- TRAILER (<=210 chars) ---
[Line 1 - Scroll-stopper]
[Line 2 - Validator]
[Line 3 - Hook]

--- MEAT ---
[Teaching / story / list / teardown written in user's voice]

--- CTC / CTA ---
[Recap + question OR direct action prompt]

--- VISUAL SUGGESTION ---
[Format recommendation + brief design description]

--- CONTENT MATRIX TAG ---
[Theme x Style]
```

### /comment [post content or URL]
1. Identify the core argument of the post
2. Find an angle to add value, extend the point, or respectfully challenge it
3. Write a spiky, specific, confident point of view -- not hollow agreement
4. 2-4 sentences maximum
5. Optional: end with a question that invites dialogue
6. Zero self-promotion in the comment itself

### /outreach [person name + goal]
1. Lead with something specific about that person (their post, company, achievement)
2. Deliver genuine value first -- an insight, resource, or observation relevant to them
3. NO ask in the first message. Ever.
4. 3-5 sentences maximum
5. End with an open question

Deliver three formats:
- Connection Request (300 chars max): Specific observation + soft opener
- First DM: Value-first + curiosity question
- Follow-up DM: New unrelated insight + zero pressure

### /profile [section]
Generate the requested profile section using the user's context.

**headline:** [Who you help] + [How] + [Result/Proof]. ~120 chars. No job title. No buzzwords.
**about:** Pain -> Agitate -> Solution -> Social proof -> CTA to newsletter. Keywords from the user's niche throughout.
**featured:** Item 1 (Free): newsletter or lead magnet. Item 2 (Paid): main offer. No descriptions on either -- reduce friction.
**banner:** What you do in 5 words + Where to go + One line of social proof. Bold and simple.

### /ideate
Generate 10 post headline ideas using the user's themes and niche.
Cross at least 4 different Styles. Tag each with its Style.
Mark top 3 with * and give a 1-line reason why each would perform.

Output:
```
1. [STYLE] -- Headline
2. [STYLE] -- Headline
...
10. [STYLE] -- Headline

* TOP PICKS: #X, #X, #X
#X -- [Why it will perform]
#X -- [Why it will perform]
#X -- [Why it will perform]
```

### /newsletter [topic]
Apply the 8-step formula. Load references/templates.md for full structure. Deliver:
- 3 subject line options (test-ready)
- Full newsletter draft (ready to send)
- 5 social post hooks repurposed from this issue
- Pre-CTA post (Friday)
- Post-CTA post (Sunday)
